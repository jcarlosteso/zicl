Introduction

In January 2023, I started a blog about porting ZIL to Clojure. It’s something I decided to do because it ties two of my favorite things: interactive fiction, and coding —what’s more, coding in Clojure.

My initial intention for the blog was to capture every design decision in it, to make it more about the why than about the how. That didn’t last long, however. It became more of a step-by-step guide to ZIcL (Zork Implementation Clojure Language).

Eventually, like I do with almost all of my personal projects —I mean, those who I actually start—, I abandoned the idea. There were several reasons for me to drop it. Some of them even were real reasons. But the fact is, I cut the blog short, and I regret that.

So I’m going to try and reboot this. Not as a blog, but as an ongoing document which I’ll keep on Github along with my code. There is a reasoning behind that change of format, too, but for now I’ll just say I try to address some of the things that made me drop the previous iteration of this project.
